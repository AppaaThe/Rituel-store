## Design Context

### Users

**She is a woman, 25–44, US-based, $50K–$150K household income, college-educated, skincare-skeptical.** She has been burned by TikTok-hyped products before and won't buy on vibes. She already runs a 5-to-12-step skincare routine and is tired of it — she is looking for *one more thing that actually works*, not a full replacement.

Primary context of use: browsing on her phone at 8am (before work), 10pm (before bed), or on weekends. She is usually tired, usually multitasking, and she has ~3 seconds to decide whether to scroll or bounce. She lives on TikTok, Instagram, Pinterest, and Reddit (r/SkincareAddiction, r/30plusskincare). She reads Byrdie, Well+Good, The Strategist, Girls Night In.

**Job to be done:**
1. Figure out if gua sha actually does anything (skepticism test).
2. Figure out if *this* gua sha is real amethyst and not a dyed resin knock-off (quality test).
3. Figure out how to use it without bruising her face (technique anxiety).
4. Buy it without feeling influencer-scammed (trust test).

### Brand Personality

**Three words: considered, warm, evidence-based.**

- **Considered** — every word, every claim, every image is chosen. No filler, no generic wellness copy, no "glow up bestie" influencer tone. The brand speaks like a smart friend who happens to have read the research.
- **Warm** — amethyst is a violet stone, the ritual is a morning one, the photography is soft-light. This is not a clinical spa brand. It is a morning bathroom brand.
- **Evidence-based** — microcirculation, lymphatic drainage, hand-finished, 2-year warranty. Cited, specific, never overclaimed. Never "ancient Chinese wisdom" mysticism. Never "dermatologist-approved" (legally we can't claim it).

**Emotional goals:** calm confidence, credibility, quiet luxury. NOT urgency, NOT FOMO, NOT hype.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Positioning:** Closer to Saint Laurent editorial than Mount Lai wellness. Sharp, editorial, luxurious — but grounded in real products, not moody fashion abstractions.

**Theme:** Light mode, cream-forward, with amethyst violet and warm terracotta accents.

**Palette (existing brand tokens — keep):**
- `--cream` (dominant surface)
- `--espresso` (primary text)
- `--terracotta` (warm accent — CTAs, flourishes)
- `--blush` (soft italic accents, secondary highlights)
- `--stone` (muted / metadata)

**Typography (new direction):**
- **Display:** `Gloock` (Google Fonts) — high-contrast modern serif with genuinely editorial character. Used only at large sizes (H1, hero titles, section titles). Not for body. Replaces Cormorant Garamond.
- **Body:** `Satoshi` (Fontshare) — geometric sans with humanist warmth, 300/400/500/700. Replaces Jost.
- **Fallback:** Georgia (serif), system-ui (sans).

Why these: four of five premium competitors (Mount Lai, Odacité, Lanshin, Wildling) pair a serif display with a sans body. Cormorant Garamond is the reflex default every AI and template picks — it's on the impeccable banned list for that exact reason. Gloock keeps the editorial serif signal but reads differently from every other gua sha site.

**Reference brands (what good looks like):**
- **Odacité** — the science-forward serif-display + sans-body balance
- **Wildling** — premium price point justified by quiet confidence, not loud claims
- **Lanshin** — founder credibility via tight typography and restraint

**Anti-references (what we explicitly are NOT):**
- **Skin Gym** — too mass, too pink, too "gym buddy"
- **Any TikTok-native brand with neon, emoji-heavy, or streamer-style copy**
- **Generic wellness apothecary brands** with sand-and-leaves imagery and "ancient wisdom" copy
- **Anything that looks templated** — Shopify default themes, Dawn with color tokens, Stan Store aesthetic

### Design Principles

1. **Every fold must earn the scroll.** The page is a sequence of promises. If a section doesn't either answer a buying question or hint at the next answer, cut it. Her scroll depth is 26% today — every surface above that line is currency.

2. **Proof beats promise.** Cite the study, name the mechanism, show the before/after. "Clinically proven" without a citation is a red flag; "Microcirculation increase measured 6m post-application (Lee et al., 2019)" is a buying signal.

3. **One decision per screen.** Tower 28 / Kosas / Dieux all ship single-CTA heroes. We do the same. The hero has one eyebrow, one headline, one sub, one button. Trust signals earn their place — they don't compete for the tap.

4. **Editorial, not content marketing.** Headlines are written, not generated. Images are composed, not sourced. A magazine spread feel beats a product-grid feel.

5. **Mobile is the primary surface, always.** 67% of Shopify sessions are phone. Design 375px first; desktop is a scale-up, not a separate composition.

### Hard constraints

- USD currency, US primary market.
- Dropship fulfillment — never imply a warehouse, physical address, or fast shipping.
- 2-year warranty maximum; no lifetime warranty claims.
- Not France, not derm-approved, not clinically tested. See `obsidian-vault/00-ONBOARDING/03-brand-facts-and-claims.md`.
- Theme deploys via GitHub → Shopify auto-sync. Edit locally, commit, push to `main`.
