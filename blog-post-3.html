Gua Sha Before or After Moisturiser?
The Right Order Explained

BY RITUEL  ·  Skincare Education  ·  5 min read

You've got your gua sha stone. You've got your moisturiser. You're standing in front of the mirror wondering which one goes first.

It's a small question with a bigger answer than you'd expect — because getting the order wrong doesn't just reduce results, it can actually work against you.

The short answer is in the box below. But read on for the full picture, because the why matters as much as the what.

The Answer

Gua sha goes before moisturiser — on clean skin with facial oil only. Moisturiser comes after.

Why the Order Matters

Gua sha works through direct contact between the stone and your skin. The stone needs to glide — but it needs to glide on something thin and absorbent, not on a barrier cream that sits on top of the skin.

When you apply gua sha over moisturiser, two things go wrong:

First, the stone slides too easily. Moisturisers are designed to form a protective barrier. They're occlusive by nature — that's what makes them effective. But for gua sha, that slippery surface means you lose pressure and control. The stone can't engage with the tissue beneath.

Second, you're pushing product where it shouldn't go. Gua sha increases microcirculation by up to 400% in the treated area. That's also what makes it so effective at driving active ingredients deeper into the skin. If you're doing gua sha over a moisturiser loaded with heavy occlusives, silicones, or fragrances — you're pushing those deeper too.

The rule: Gua sha should be done on clean, freshly oiled skin. Oil is thin, skin-compatible, and absorbs as you work. It lubricates the stone without blocking what the technique is trying to do. Moisturiser seals everything in — it belongs at the end.

The Complete Morning Routine Order

Here's exactly how to sequence your routine when gua sha is part of it:

01

Cleanse

Start with clean skin. Gua sha on skin that has last night's skincare on it defeats the purpose — you're just moving product around.

02
Optional

Toner / Essence

If you use a water-based toner or essence, apply it before the oil. These absorb quickly and won't interfere with the gua sha technique.

03
Gua Sha Step

Facial Oil

2–3 drops warmed in your palms, pressed into clean skin. This is what the stone glides on. Without it, the stone drags and causes irritation.

04
Gua Sha Step

Gua Sha

5 minutes. Upward strokes, light pressure. The oil is still fresh on the skin — this is the window where the technique works best.

05
Optional

Ice Roller

Immediately after gua sha, the ice roller locks in the sculpted effect, closes pores, and reduces any residual redness. 60–90 seconds.

06

Serum

Now apply any active serums — vitamin C, niacinamide, retinol (evening). The increased circulation from gua sha means these absorb more effectively than at any other point in your routine.

07

Moisturiser

Seal everything in. Now the moisturiser barrier is working in your favour — locking in the oil, the serums, and the effects of the technique.

08

SPF (Morning Only)

Always last. SPF is a barrier, not a treatment — it goes on top of everything else.

Why Oil and Not Another Product?

People ask whether they can use a serum or a light lotion instead of oil for the gua sha step. The answer is usually no — here's why.

🫙

Facial Oil

Thin, skin-compatible, absorbs as you work. Provides enough slip without forming a barrier. Delivers active ingredients as circulation increases. The right choice.

💧

Serum

Water-based serums absorb too quickly — by the time you start your second stroke, the skin is already dry. You'll need to reapply constantly, which disrupts the technique.

🧴

Moisturiser

Too thick and occlusive. Creates a barrier that prevents proper engagement. Can push heavy ingredients deeper with the increased blood flow — not ideal.

❌

No Product

Dry skin and a stone tool is a bad combination. The friction causes irritation, broken capillaries, and bruising. Always use oil.

🪨

What About Evening Routines?

The same order applies at night — cleanse, oil, gua sha, then the rest of your routine. The main difference is what follows.

Evening is actually an excellent time for gua sha if you're using active ingredients like retinol or AHAs. The increased circulation drives these deeper and makes them more effective. Apply the active after gua sha, not before or during.

One caveat: if you're using a prescription retinoid or a strong exfoliant, don't do gua sha on the same evening on days when your skin is already sensitised. The combination of chemical exfoliation and mechanical stimulation can be too much for some skin types.

Common Mistakes with Routine Order

Doing gua sha over SPF. This is the most common mistake. Some people do a quick gua sha "touch up" before going out, over their morning SPF. Don't. You're pushing SPF into your skin and breaking down the protective barrier at the same time.

Using retinol oil as your gua sha oil. Retinol is photosensitive and already penetrates well on its own. Using it as your gua sha medium at high concentrations can cause irritation. Stick to a plain facial oil like rosehip, squalane, or jojoba for the gua sha step, and apply any active oils separately.

Waiting too long after cleansing. Your skin is most receptive right after cleansing — pores are open, surface is clear. The longer you wait between cleansing and gua sha, the less optimal the window. Get into the habit of going straight from cleanse to oil to stone.

The simple rule to remember: gua sha goes on bare, oiled skin — after cleansing, before everything else. Think of it as a physical treatment that preps your skin to absorb everything that follows.
