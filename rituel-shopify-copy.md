**Page title:** FAQ | RITUEL

---

**Does gua sha actually work?**
Yes — if you do it consistently. Gua sha physically moves fluid in your lymphatic system and stimulates facial muscles. The results are cumulative. Most customers notice reduced puffiness from the first week. Jawline definition becomes visible around weeks 2-3. Skin texture improvements build over months of daily use.

**How long does it take to see results?**
Reduced morning puffiness: 1-3 days.
Visible jawline definition: 2-3 weeks of daily use.
Skin texture and tone improvements: 4-8 weeks.
The key is consistency — 5 minutes every morning, not 30 minutes once a week.

**Do I need to use the facial oil?**
We strongly recommend it. Gua sha on dry skin drags and can cause friction — which defeats the purpose. The facial oil creates the glide the stone needs and amplifies results because your skin is primed to absorb active ingredients immediately after a gua sha session. If you don't have our oil, use your regular moisturiser or a few drops of any facial oil.

**Can I use gua sha if I have acne?**
Yes — with care. Avoid active breakouts (pimples, cysts, inflamed spots). Work around them. Gua sha can actually help with overall skin clarity over time by improving circulation and lymphatic drainage. If you have severe cystic acne, speak to a dermatologist first.

**How do I clean my gua sha stone?**
After each use, spritz with our Stone Cleansing Spray and let air dry. Alternatively, rinse gently with mild soap and cool water — avoid hot water, which can crack natural stone. Never soak your stone or put it in the dishwasher.

**How often should I do the ritual?**
Every morning. The lymphatic system responds to consistent, daily stimulation. Sporadic use gives sporadic results. 5 minutes every morning is the practice that creates change.

**Is gua sha safe for all skin types?**
Yes — including sensitive skin. Use light pressure, always move upward and outward, and never use on broken, sunburned, or actively inflamed skin.

**Rule 2: Less pressure than you think.**
The stone should glide, not drag. If your skin is moving with the stone instead of the stone moving over your skin, lighten your grip. Think: you're moving fluid under the skin, not scraping the surface.
